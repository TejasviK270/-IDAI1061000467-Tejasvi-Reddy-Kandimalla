RESEARCH SUMMARY — SCENARIO 1: MINDFUL WORK APP

Background:
Over 60% of employees worldwide experience daily work-related stressors that impact their productivity, motivation, and mental health. Workplace burnout is at an all-time high, with the World Health Organization officially classifying burnout as an occupational phenomenon. Traditional stress management tools — such as scheduled workshops, general wellness programs, or basic meditation apps — fail to provide instant, personalized relief tailored to individual needs.

Research Methods Used:
- Online survey distributed to 10 participants (mix of IT professionals, managers, interns, and HR staff)
- Informal interviews with 3 office employees and 1 wellness coach
- Observation of employee behavior during high-stress periods (before meetings, deadlines)

Key Findings:
1. 8 out of 10 employees reported feeling stressed at least 3 times per week.
2. The most common stress triggers were: tight deadlines (90%), back-to-back meetings (70%), and difficult team communication (60%).
3. 7 out of 10 employees said they did not have a private, calm space to decompress at work.
4. Preferred relaxation methods included: listening to music (80%), deep breathing (60%), short walks (50%), and aromatherapy (30%).
5. Most employees said they could only spare 5–15 minutes for a stress-relief session during work hours.
6. Younger employees (under 30) preferred tech-driven solutions (apps, wearables), while older employees preferred guided, step-by-step relaxation.
7. 6 out of 10 employees said that being able to book a pod session in advance would reduce their anxiety about finding a relaxation space.
8. Employees valued privacy and personalization above all other features in a stress-relief tool.

Conclusion:
There is a clear need for an intelligent, private, and personalized stress-relief solution that fits within a short work break. The Smart Stress Relief Pod App addresses this by combining sensory personalization, AI-driven recommendations, and scheduled booking into one seamless experience.
