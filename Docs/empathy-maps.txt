EMPATHY MAP 1: Riya Sharma (IT Developer, 28)

THINKS & FEELS:
- "I have no space to breathe between meetings."
- Feels overwhelmed by the volume of tasks.
- Fears falling behind on sprint goals.
- Secretly envious of colleagues who seem calm under pressure.

SEES:
- A busy open-plan office with constant movement and noise.
- Colleagues also looking stressed and hunched over laptops.
- Wellness posters on the wall that no one pays attention to.
- Notification badges piling up on her screen.

SAYS & DOES:
- Says: "I'm fine" when asked how she is.
- Puts on headphones to signal she doesn't want to be disturbed.
- Skips lunch breaks to catch up on work.
- Drinks 4+ cups of coffee daily.

HEARS:
- Manager saying "can you finish this by EOD?"
- Team Slack pings going off constantly.
- Colleagues complaining about deadlines.
- Background office chatter and phone calls.

PAIN POINTS: No private space, constant interruptions, no personalized relaxation tool.
GOALS: Quick mental reset, mood uplift, privacy.

EMPATHY MAP 2: Aarav Mehta (Marketing Manager, 35)

THINKS & FEELS:
- "What if I freeze during the presentation?"
- Anxiety about being judged by senior leaders.
- Feels responsible for the whole team's performance.
- Frustrated that his current coping methods (coffee) make things worse.

SEES:
- Conference rooms always booked.
- Team looking to him for direction even when he's stressed.
- Competitors' campaigns succeeding online.
- His own calendar blocked back-to-back.

SAYS & DOES:
- Takes deep breaths at his desk, visibly nervous.
- Over-prepares for presentations late into the night.
- Snaps at team members unintentionally when overwhelmed.
- Drinks coffee before stressful meetings even knowing it heightens anxiety.

HEARS:
- "The client wants changes by tomorrow."
- Positive feedback is rare; most communication is urgent or corrective.
- Background meeting noise in open plan office.

PAIN POINTS: No structured, immediate stress relief before high-stakes events.
GOALS: Guided breathing, a calm space, and something to do 15 minutes before a presentation.

EMPATHY MAP 3: Kavita Rao (Operations Director, 52)

THINKS & FEELS:
- "No one understands the pressure I carry."
- Feels isolated in her leadership role.
- Frustrated by the lack of recognition for her effort.
- Worried about burnout affecting her health.

SEES:
- Problems escalated to her that should have been resolved below.
- A large team that depends on her decisions.
- Little time on her calendar marked as "free."
- Wellness programs that seem aimed at younger employees.

SAYS & DOES:
- Puts on a calm exterior in meetings.
- Delegates tasks but follows up constantly.
- Attends weekend yoga — the only time she truly relaxes.
- Declines social events due to exhaustion.

HEARS:
- Urgent WhatsApp messages from vendors even after hours.
- Board members requesting status updates.
- Team conflicts she must mediate.

PAIN POINTS: No senior-appropriate, dignified stress-relief option at work.
GOALS: A genuinely restorative, private 15-minute pod session she can plan into her day.
